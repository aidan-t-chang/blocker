A problem that I found with most app blockers is that they stop you immediately from looking at distracting websites. At this point, your brain is hardwired for that dopamine hit, so you just find a way around the blocker and end up on the same website again. SmartBlocker is different. Now, you can still get that dopamine hit but still stay on task. With time off task being no more than one minute (I recommend 5 seconds!), you can still get that dopamine hit but not enough to get you off task.

How to Use SmartBlocker

1. Toggle the power switch to enable or disable blocking.

2. To add a URL to block, enter it in the input field and click "Add URL". You can use wildcards like * to block multiple URLs (e.g., youtube.com/*).

3. To set a time limit for blocking, enter the number of seconds in the "Close after" field and click "Save". This will automatically block URLs after the specified time.

4. The list of currently blocked URLs is displayed at the bottom. You can remove any URL from the list by clicking on them.
